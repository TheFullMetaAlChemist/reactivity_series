Year 10 Chemistry Consolidation Worksheet

Metal Oxides, Reactivity Series, and Metal Extraction

Section 1: Metal Oxides and Oxidation-Reduction

1. Define oxidation and reduction in terms of oxygen.

Hint: Think about whether oxygen is gained or lost.
Answer: Oxidation is the gain of oxygen, and reduction is the loss of oxygen.

2. Write a balanced chemical equation for the reaction of magnesium with oxygen.

Hint: Magnesium forms a metal oxide when it reacts with oxygen.
Answer: \( 2Mg + O_2 \rightarrow 2MgO \)

3. Explain why the reaction between a metal and oxygen is considered an oxidation reaction.

Hint: What happens to the metal in terms of oxygen?
Answer: The metal gains oxygen, which is the definition of oxidation.

4. Identify which substance is oxidized and which is reduced in the following reaction:

\( 2CuO + C \rightarrow 2Cu + CO_2 \)

Hint: Look at the oxygen changes.
Answer: Copper oxide is reduced (loses oxygen), and carbon is oxidized (gains oxygen).

5. True or False: Reduction is the gain of oxygen.

Hint: Think about how oxidation and reduction are opposites.
Answer: False.

Section 2: The Reactivity Series

6. Define the reactivity series.

Hint: It ranks metals based on their reactions.
Answer: A list of metals arranged in order of their reactivity.

7. List the following metals in order of reactivity, from most to least reactive: zinc, potassium, iron, calcium, copper.

Hint: Consider how these metals react with water and acids.
Answer: Potassium, calcium, zinc, iron, copper.

8. Why is hydrogen included in the reactivity series, even though it is not a metal?

Hint: Look at displacement reactions in acids.
Answer: Hydrogen is included because it helps compare the reactivity of metals in acid reactions.

9. Explain why a more reactive metal can displace a less reactive metal from its compound. Provide an example reaction.

Hint: Think about competition for oxygen or other atoms.
Answer: A more reactive metal takes the place of a less reactive metal in a compound, e.g., \( Zn + CuSO_4 \rightarrow ZnSO_4 + Cu \).

10. Complete the word equation for the reaction of sodium with water:

\[ \_\_\_ + \_\_\_ \rightarrow \_\_\_ + \_\_\_ \]

Hint: Sodium reacts vigorously with water to form a hydroxide and a gas.
Answer: Sodium + Water \( \rightarrow \) Sodium Hydroxide + Hydrogen.

Section 3: Metal Extraction and Reduction

11. What is the main method used to extract metals that are less reactive than carbon?

Hint: Consider how carbon interacts with metal oxides.
Answer: Reduction using carbon.

12. Explain why gold does not need to be extracted using a chemical reaction.

Hint: Think about how gold is found in nature.
Answer: Gold is unreactive and found in its native form.

13. Write a balanced equation for the extraction of iron from iron(III) oxide using carbon monoxide.

Hint: The reaction forms iron and a gas.
Answer: \( Fe_2O_3 + 3CO \rightarrow 2Fe + 3CO_2 \)

14. Describe how carbon is used to extract metals from their oxides.

Hint: Think about what happens in a blast furnace.
Answer: Carbon removes oxygen from metal oxides, leaving behind the pure metal.

15. Evaluate the environmental impact of using carbon to extract metals. Give at least two points.

Hint: Consider pollution and energy use.
Answer: 1) Produces CO₂, contributing to climate change.
2) Mining for carbon-based fuels can damage ecosystems.

Section 4: Application and Exam-Style Questions

16. A student places a strip of magnesium into a solution of copper sulfate. Explain what happens in terms of reactivity and write a balanced equation for the reaction.

Hint: Magnesium is more reactive than copper.
Answer: Magnesium displaces copper, forming magnesium sulfate and copper metal.
\( Mg + CuSO_4 \rightarrow MgSO_4 + Cu \)

17. Iron reacts with dilute hydrochloric acid to produce a gas. Write a word equation and a balanced symbol equation for this reaction.

Hint: The gas produced is hydrogen.
Answer:
Word equation: Iron + Hydrochloric Acid → Iron Chloride + Hydrogen
Symbol equation: \( Fe + 2HCl \rightarrow FeCl_2 + H_2 \)

18. Describe an experiment to determine the reactivity of four unknown metals. What observations would indicate the most and least reactive metals?

Hint: Consider reactions with acids and water.
Answer: Place the metals in acid and/or water and observe the production of bubbles (hydrogen gas). More vigorous bubbling indicates a more reactive metal, while little or no bubbling suggests a less reactive metal.

19. Explain why aluminium appears to be unreactive but is actually a reactive metal.

Hint: Think about protective coatings.
Answer: Aluminium has an oxide layer that prevents further reaction.

20. Why is electrolysis used to extract very reactive metals like aluminium instead of using carbon?

Hint: Look at the reactivity series.
Answer: Aluminium is more reactive than carbon, so carbon cannot reduce it.

Challenge Question

21. Metals are crucial for modern technology. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of recycling metals instead of extracting them from ores.

Hint: Consider environmental, economic, and resource availability factors.
Answer:
  • Advantages: Reduces mining damage, saves energy, decreases pollution.
  • Disadvantages: Requires sorting and processing, high initial recycling costs.
